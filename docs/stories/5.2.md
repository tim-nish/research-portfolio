# Story 5.2: Site-canonical article rendering (markdown body → page)

Canonical discussion record: https://github.com/tim-nish/website/issues/66
(part of umbrella #64 — `docs/article-publishing-spec.md` §1, site-canonical
mode). On conflict, the issue wins.

As a reader, I want each valid site-canonical projection rendered as a full
article page on the site, so that site-canonical articles are readable at
their canonical route instead of being link-out records.

## Acceptance criteria

1. **Given** a valid projection file, **when** the site builds, **then** its
   markdown body renders as an HTML page at the article's canonical route
   with the frontmatter `title` as the page title.
2. **Given** the #25 canonical article page template's links-only
   assumption, **when** this story lands, **then** a site-canonical article
   renders its full body while an external article never does.
3. **Given** a committed projection, **when** the build runs, **then** the
   body is rendered verbatim — no build-time fetching, rewriting, or
   enrichment from the authoring side.
4. **Given** a committed sample projection, **when** built end-to-end,
   **then** the page renders; a body edited only in this repo is a defect
   per the spec's precedence rule (enforced by 5.4's lint, not blocked
   here).
