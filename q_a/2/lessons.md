Treat article structure as a schema problem: fill-in frameworks with mandatory evidence slots make quality structural instead of inspiration-dependent.
Limit the article category set to genres your own evidence can feed (project introductions, engineering lessons, evaluation methodology, research surveys) and exclude generic tutorials explicitly.
Gate each article category on an entry condition (a shipped release, a real failure artifact, a measurement you ran) so the framework doubles as a go/no-go check.
Invert the human/AI division of labor when drafting from existing materials: AI extracts facts with source pointers; the human supplies judgment through a bounded interview.
Cap AI-to-human questions (≤5) and accept bullet answers to keep human attention cost fixed and predictable.
Require every AI-drafted claim to be source-pointed, interview-sourced, or explicitly marked unverified — never silently asserted.
Route repeated rewrites back to a new interview question instead of open-ended editing to bound drafting churn.
Treat a published article as an artifact and the review cycle count — not review quality — as the process cost to minimize.
Spend tokens on the strongest model at drafting time; run reviews as single bounded passes on cheap models.
Order review passes structure-before-prose because structural changes invalidate prose feedback.
Move every mechanically checkable review criterion (frontmatter, links, title length, heading density) into a zero-token lint script.
Ask reviewers for capped, severity-tagged findings with suggested fixes — never rewrites — and forbid praise and summary in review output.
Add a context-free cold read to catch missing-context defects that any grounded reviewer is structurally blind to.
Ground AI reviews in the repository so factual claims are checked against sources, which matters more than the reviewing model's brand.
State each article's single claim in the title and keep one idea per article; split anything with two.
End every article with the same pointer block to your own domain — it is what converts platform reach into owned audience.
Version published articles openly instead of perfecting drafts privately; a registry-driven site makes updates routine.
Check which published pieces actually pull (platform stats, Search Console) before choosing the next topic.
